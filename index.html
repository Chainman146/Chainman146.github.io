About His life

  • 1912: Born in Michigan

  • 1922: Moved To Los Angeles

  • 1937: Recieved PhD in Chemistry (recieved bachelors in 1933)

  • 1942: Joined the Manhattan Project

    1942: Married Helen Griggs

  • 1946: Became Professor in Chemistry

  • 1958-1961: Became Chancellor Of California Berkeley

  • 1961-1971: Chairman Of the Atomic Energy Commission

  • 1972: Became President of the American Association for the Advancement of Science

  • 1976: Became President of the American Chemical Society

  • Lived his later life in Lafayette, California Where he published, and edited journals.

  • 1999: Died due to a stroke, in Boston, Massachusetts

His Achievements

Seaborg was on the team for 10 elements: Plutonium, Americium, Curium, Berkelium, Californium, Einsteinium, Fermium, Mendelevium, Nobelium, and Seaborgium, which was named in his honor. 3 of which he was credited with lead discoverer: Americium, Curium, and Berkelium. The rest of which he was credited Co-Discoverer. Seaborg, And Oganession are the only 2 living people to have an element named after them.

Discovered Iron-59 Which was useful for blood studies, and Iodine-131 Which is used to treat thyroid disease. This even saved his own Mother's life!

in 1980, He converted thousands of atoms of Bismuth-209 Into Gold-197, by removing protons and neutrons.

He won a Nobel Prize in 1951 for his achievements.
